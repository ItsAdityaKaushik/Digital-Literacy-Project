Cyberbullying is a form of online harassment where individuals are targeted through digital platforms such as social media, messaging apps, or online forums. It involves behaviors like posting harmful comments, spreading false information, sharing private content, or repeatedly targeting someone to cause emotional distress.

In a typical scenario, a college student becomes a victim of cyberbullying after a minor disagreement with classmates. The situation escalates when a group of students starts posting negative comments and memes about the individual on social media. They may also create fake accounts to anonymously continue the harassment, making it difficult to identify the source.

As the harassment continues, the victim begins to feel stressed, anxious, and socially isolated. They may avoid using social media or participating in online discussions. Over time, this can affect their mental well-being, self-confidence, and academic performance.

Cyberbullying commonly targets students and young individuals who are highly active online. The anonymity provided by digital platforms often encourages such behavior without immediate consequences.

This case highlights how online actions can have serious real-life impacts. It emphasizes the importance of responsible digital behavior, awareness, and timely reporting to prevent and address cyberbullying effectively.
